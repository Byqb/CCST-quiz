Question 9: IPv6 Address TypesQuestion 9 of 71

Which IPv6 address would be assigned by DHCPv6 to a host that needs to reach IPv6-only resources on the internet?
Correct Answer: A. 2001:1db8:1234::1.
To reach the public internet, a host needs a globally routable address. Addresses in the 2000::/3 range are Global Unicast Addresses (GUA). `fe80::` is a non-routable Link-Local address, `fd...` is a non-routable Unique Local Address, and `ff...` is a Multicast address.
